Greetings Adventurer!, I'm here to introduce you to the wonderful world of squares!

A circle has a perimeter
length, area, and side length.
The perimeter is the total distance
around the outside of a shape.
Think of it like tracing the border
of a shape with a piece of
string—how long is the string?

To find the perimeter, you
have to use the formula:
Perimeter = 4 × side

An area of a square is the amount of
space inside the shape. Formula for area:
Area = side × side

To find the the side length of a square:
Side = √Area

Example: You have a square garden, and you
know that the area of the garden is
64 square meters.
1. Find the side length of the square garden.
2. Calculate the perimeter of the garden.
3. Calculate the area again to verify your result.

Solution:
side = √Area, side = √64 side = 8 meters
Perimeter = 4 × side, Perimeter = 4 × 8
Perimeter = 32 meters
Area = side × side, Area = 8 × 8
Area = 64 square meters
